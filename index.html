Biography

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most accomplished cricketers of his generation. Known for his aggressive batting style and strong leadership, Kohli has established himself as a modern cricketing legend. Kohli's cricketing journey began at a young age, and he quickly rose through the ranks of junior cricket in Delhi. His talent and dedication were evident early on, and he soon caught the attention of selectors. Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008, in an ODI against Sri Lanka. His Test debut followed in 2011, against the West Indies. One of Kohli's defining traits as a cricketer is his incredible consistency. He has a remarkable ability to score runs across all formats of the game. His aggressive approach to batting, combined with his strong technique and mental toughness, has made him a nightmare for bowlers around the world. Kohli has broken numerous records over the years, including becoming the fastest player to reach 10,000 ODI runs. In addition to his batting prowess, Kohli is also known for his leadership skills. He has served as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats and has led the team to numerous victories. Under his captaincy, India achieved a historic Test series win in Australia in 2018-2019, becoming the first Asian team to do so. Off the field, Kohli is known for his philanthropic work and his commitment to fitness. He is a vocal advocate for healthy living and has inspired many with his fitness regimen. Kohli is also involved in various charitable initiatives, including his foundation, which focuses on empowering underprivileged children. Kohli's personal life has also been a topic of interest for many. He married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in 2017, and the couple welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika, in 2021. As Kohli continues to play at the highest level, his impact on the game of cricket is undeniable. He has set new standards for excellence and has inspired a generation of cricketers with his performances. Kohli's journey from a talented young cricketer to a global icon is a testament to his hard work, determination, and passion for the game.
